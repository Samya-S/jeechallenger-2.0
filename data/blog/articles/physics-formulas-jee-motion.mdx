Understanding Laws of Motion is crucial for JEE. This guide covers essential formulas with mathematical rigor needed for both JEE Main and Advanced.

## Newton's Laws of Motion

### First Law (Law of Inertia)
A body at rest stays at rest, and a body in motion continues in motion with constant velocity unless acted upon by an external force.

### Second Law
The fundamental equation of motion:

$$F = ma$$

Where:
- $F$ = Net force (Newton)
- $m$ = Mass (kg)
- $a$ = Acceleration (m/s²)

**Important Derivation:**

$$F = \frac{dp}{dt} = \frac{d(mv)}{dt}$$

For constant mass: $F = m\frac{dv}{dt} = ma$

### Third Law
For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction:

$$F_{AB} = -F_{BA}$$

## Kinematic Equations

### For Uniform Acceleration

The three fundamental equations:

1. **Velocity-Time Relation:**
$$v = u + at$$

2. **Position-Time Relation:**
$$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$

3. **Velocity-Position Relation:**
$$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$

Where:
- $u$ = Initial velocity
- $v$ = Final velocity
- $a$ = Acceleration
- $t$ = Time
- $s$ = Displacement

### nth Second Displacement

Distance covered in the nth second:

$$s_n = u + \frac{a}{2}(2n-1)$$

## Friction

### Static Friction
Maximum static friction before motion begins:

$$f_s \leq \mu_s N$$

Where $\mu_s$ is the coefficient of static friction.

### Kinetic Friction
Once the object is moving:

$$f_k = \mu_k N$$

Where $\mu_k$ is the coefficient of kinetic friction.

**Key Point:** Always, $\mu_s > \mu_k$

## Circular Motion

### Centripetal Force

For uniform circular motion:

$$F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} = m\omega^2r$$

Where:
- $r$ = Radius of circular path
- $\omega$ = Angular velocity
- $v$ = Linear velocity

**Relationship:** $v = r\omega$

### Banking of Roads

Optimal angle for no friction required:

$$\tan\theta = \frac{v^2}{rg}$$

## Work-Energy Theorem

The work done by all forces equals change in kinetic energy:

$$W = \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2}m(v^2 - u^2)$$

### Work Done by Variable Force

$$W = \int_{x_1}^{x_2} F \cdot dx$$

## Power

Average power:

$$P_{avg} = \frac{W}{t}$$

Instantaneous power:

$$P = F \cdot v = Fv\cos\theta$$

## Impulse-Momentum Theorem

Impulse equals change in momentum:

$$J = \Delta p = F \cdot \Delta t$$

For variable force:

$$J = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} F \cdot dt$$

## Practice Problems

### Problem 1: Free Fall
A stone is dropped from height $h = 45m$. Find velocity when it hits ground. (Take $g = 10 m/s^2$)

**Solution:**
Using $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$

$$v^2 = 0 + 2(10)(45) = 900$$
$$v = 30 \text{ m/s}$$

### Problem 2: Inclined Plane
Block of mass $m = 5kg$ slides down a frictionless incline of angle $\theta = 30°$. Find acceleration.

**Solution:**
Component of gravity along incline:

$$a = g\sin\theta = 10 \times \sin(30°) = 5 \text{ m/s}^2$$
